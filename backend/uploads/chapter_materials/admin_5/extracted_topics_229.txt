- Grasy goes to a garden: “Grasy goes to a garden.”  
- Peter picks pebbles from a pot: “Peter picks pebbles from a pot.”  

2. Let’s Describe a Picture  
- Garden scene description: “Here is a picture of a garden. Look! There is a tortoise in the corner. There are frogs in the lake. They are green. There are two rabbits. How cute! They are playing with a girl. Look! A girl and a boy are playing with puppies. Can you find a baby crocodile in the picture? Yes. It’s on this boy’s shoulder. Where are the squirrels? This girl has two squirrels, one is on her hand; one is on her head. Who has a cap on his head? This boy has a cap on his head. What are they doing? They are playing.”  

3. Classroom Interaction Activities  
- Rabbit question: “Teacher: Show the rabbit. What does it have? Students: Here’s the rabbit. It has a carrot.”  
- Cat colour question: “Teacher: Touch the cat. What colour is it? Students: Black.”  
- Counting trees: “Teacher: Count the trees. How many trees are there? Students: Six.”  
- Locating the sheep: “Teacher: Touch the sheep. Where is the sheep? Students: Yes. It is near the house.”  
- Animal identification: “Teacher: What is this? (cock, hen, chick, donkey) Students …”  

4. Story Time – Who Flies the Highest?  
- Story opening: “Once two crows fought. Both said, ‘I can fly the highest.’ They had a race. Each crow would carry a bag as high as it could.”  
- Bag contents: “The first crow filled his bag with cotton. The other crow filled his bag with salt.”  
- Rain event: “Soon it began to rain. The salt began to dissolve in the second crow’s bag. The first crow had cotton in the bag. The cotton absorbed the rain water and became heavy.”  
- Outcome: “The second crow won the challenge.”  

5. Beginning a Narrative  
- Story starter example: “‘There was a big forest…’ is a possible way to begin a story.”
